Personal Profile

Dr. Steven M. Drucker has a 30+ year career in computer research and development focusing on graphics, interfaces, and information visualization. Developed prototypes, products, and websites; managed several groups; published over 65 papers; and filed over 120 patents. He is currently a Principal Researcher in the Visual Interaction in Business and Entertainment (vibe) group atMicrosoft Research (MSR) focusing on human computer interaction for dealing with large amounts of information and an affiliate professor at the University of Washington Computer Science and Engineering Department. His research is at the intersection of user interface and information, examining how user interaction transforms conventional media or helps us understand and communicate about inforrmation. Before coming to Microsoft, he received his Ph.D. from the Computer Graphics and Animation Group at the MIT Media Lab in May 1994. His thesis research was on intelligent camera control interfaces for graphical environments. Dr. Drucker graduated Magna Cum Laude with Honors in Neurosciences from Brown University where he also worked with the Brown Graphics Group and went on to complete his masters at the Artificial Intelligence Laboratory at MIT doing research in robot learning. He has demonstrated his work on stage with Satya Nadella at the Microsoft CEO Summit and with Bill Gates at the Consumer Electronics Show (CES); has shipped numerous products including software on the web for visualizing large datasets and gathering and acting on information collected on the web; was written up in the New York Times; filed over 108 patents; and published papers on technologies as diverse as exploratory search, information visualization, multi-user environments, online social interaction, hypermedia research, human and robot perceptual capabilities, robot learning, parallel computer graphics, spectator oriented gaming, and human interfaces for camera control.
